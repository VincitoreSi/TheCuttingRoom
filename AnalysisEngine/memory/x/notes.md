# X (Twitter) video — craft notes

- X clips are often lower-production and screen-recording / talking-head / repost style; judge
  `likely_ai_generated` and `visual_style` accordingly rather than assuming polished studio work.
- Captions/context frequently live in the tweet text, not burned into the frame — keep
  `text_overlays` limited to what is actually ON SCREEN.
- Audio is commonly original dialogue or a raw clip rather than a licensed trending sound; lean
  toward `audio_type: voiceover_led` or `music_only` unless a clear track is present.
- Aspect ratios vary more than IG (16:9, 1:1, 9:16 all appear) — read the real frame, don't
  default to vertical.
- Retention devices skew toward a strong verbal claim or a controversial hook in the first
  seconds; capture the spoken hook verbatim.
